Passenger Transport Split Between Cars, Buses, and Rail

This visualisation uses 2019 passenger transport data from the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) which is an intergovournmental organisation consistig of 38 countries, most of which are regarded as developed countries with high-income economies.

The OECD data uses the measurement unit of Passenger-Kilometres (PASKM), which represents the transport of on epassenger over one kilomtre.

Carbon Emissions for Each Mode of Transport

Emmisions form vehicles are shown in grams of carbon dioxide equavalent emitted per passenger-kilometre traveled, where carbon dioxide equavalent (CO2eq) is a unit that combines the global warming potential of all of the emissions formed in the running of the vehicle into one eisily comparable number.

Some forms of transport are more eficient, from an emissions perspective than others, with trains being the most eficient and cars being the least eficient as seen in the figure to the right. These values are, however, based on averages and will change from country to country, as they depend on the cleanliness of the electricity grid, what percentage of the countries passenger vehicles are electric, and so on.
