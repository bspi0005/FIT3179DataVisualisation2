Passenger Transport

The Split Between Cars, Buses, and Rail

This visualisation uses 2019 passenger transport data from the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) which is an intergovournmental organisation consistig of 38 countries, most of which are regarded as developed countries with high-income economies.

Travel Breakdown by Transport Type

The OECD data uses the measurement unit of Passenger-Kilometres (PASKM), which represents the transport of one passenger over one kilomtre. The Passenger-Kilometres unit allows the transport data to be normalised by how many passengers are transported in one vehicle, leading better and more representitive concluseions being made from the rescorded data.

Carbon Emissions for Each Transport Type

Emmisions form vehicles are shown in grams of carbon dioxide equavalent emitted per passenger-kilometre traveled, where carbon dioxide equavalent (CO2eq) is a unit that combines the global warming potential of all of the emissions formed in the running of the vehicle into one eisily comparable number.

Some forms of transport are more polluting than others, with trains being the cleanest and cars being the most polluting as seen in the figure below.
